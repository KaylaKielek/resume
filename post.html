Course Reflections

What I've learned from what I've done

Education Course Key Takeaways

Education 1231: Professional Dimensions I

Grade 5 Field Placement: Each student is unique and learns in a different way. This is why teaching needs to be learner-centered.

Education 1233: Professional Dimensions II

Grade 5/6 Field Placement: Lessons and units should be created using the concept of backwards design.

EDUC 2321 Education & Individual Development

Student motivation is a key aspect of fostering learning and higher-level thinking.

EDUC 2325 Current & Emerging Technology

Technologies, such as SMART Boards or online programs can aid in teaching as well as student understanding.

EDUC 2371 Language Development and Literacy

Grade 5 Field Placement: Language can develop through all subjects, not just Language Arts.

EDUC 2375 Measurement & Evaluation

Grade 1 Field Placement: Teachers should use assessment in various ways including: for learning, as learning, and of learning.

EDUC 3010 Practicum I

Building strong relationships with students helps to create a safe and fun learning environment.

EDUC 3102 Teaching Drama

The ‘Mantle of the Expert’ drama strategy can be used to put students in an ‘expert’ role to explore different aspects of curriculum in various subjects.

EDUC 3103 Teaching ELA

Daily 5 can help students become independent in their learning as well as personalize stations to differentiate for students.

EDUC 3106 Teaching Science

Inquiry-based learning helps students to think critically, ask thought provoking questions, and reach a deep understanding of the world around them.

EDUC 3108 Teaching Mathematics

Teach a diverse set of ways to tackle math. Teach to each student’s needs.

EDUC 3351 Aboriginal Cultural Dimensions

It is important to teach students about Canada’s past and people. By teaching students about Canada’s wrongful actions towards indigenous people, students will learn to shape the future in a more positive and inclusive light.

EDUC 3361 Exceptional Students

Students are all unique and have different needs. It is important, as an educators, to teach to a student’s exceptionalities and passions in order to promote lifelong learning.

EDUC 4030 Practicum II

Students need engaging, hands-on activities that work to promote a passion for learning and further exploration of thoughts and ideas.

EDUC 4101 Teaching Art

Art is not just creating, it is expressing.

EDUC 4104 Teaching Music

Assessment of the arts should focus on the process, not the product.

EDUC 4105 Teaching Physical Education

Physical Education is not just about teaching fundamental movement skills. It is about providing opportunities to explore various sports and activities that help to build a student’s identity.

EDUC 4107 Teaching Social Studies

Social Studies should be taught using the world around us. By providing real world experiences, students can connect to their learning and reach higher-level thinking.

EDUC 4201 Int. Ideas, Values and Praxis

Play-based learning is beneficial in kindergarten because it gives students the opportunity to explore different concepts and ideas while interacting with their peers and building crucial social skills.

EDUC 4471 ESL in Mainstream

A classroom should celebrate multiculturalism. Providing students with opportunities to showcase their culture will help foster a safe, caring, and diverse community.



Humanities Course Key Takeaways

ENGL 305 Literature for Children

There are various types of children’s literature, ranging from board books to chapter books. It is important to expose children to a variety of age-appropriate literature as they grow.

ENGL 325 Shakespeare II

Many of William Shakespeare’s plays, such as Hamlet and Othello, have deep, underlying meanings that reveal different perspectives on life and humanity.

HIST 3361 The Holocaust

Culture and Identity is an important human right. We cannot let history repeat.

ENGL 2210 English Lit to Restoration

Classic tales such as Beowulf, mark the beginnings of children’s literature.

RELS 204 Intro to World Religions

Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, Confucianism, and Taoism all have ritual practices and beliefs that make them an important part of history as well as the present.

PHIL 1101 Philosophy: Knowledge & Exis

Information from a single source should not be taken as truth. As people, we must question the world around us and seek information in order to make us well-rounded in our identities.

HIST 1131 Canada, Origins to 1867

Indigenous peoples were the first to live in Canada and continue to act as an important aspect of Canadian culture and identity.
